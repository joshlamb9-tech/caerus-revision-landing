Why most revision doesn’t work — and how we fix it

The design of Caerus Latin is grounded in cognitive science research on how memory actually works.

📉

The Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve

Hermann Ebbinghaus (1885) demonstrated that without review, we forget roughly 70% of new material within 24 hours. A single session of vocabulary reading does almost nothing — unless followed by spaced retrieval.

Ebbinghaus, H. (1885). Über das Gedächtnis. Leipzig: Duncker.
🔁

Spaced Repetition

The spacing effect — studying material across increasing intervals — is one of the most robust findings in cognitive psychology. Caerus Latin tracks each word's score and surfaces the ones most at risk of being forgotten.

Cepeda, N.J. et al. (2006). Distributed practice in verbal recall tasks. Psychological Bulletin, 132(3), 354–380.
🧠

The Testing Effect

Roediger & Karpicke (2006) showed that retrieving information strengthens memory far more than re-reading it. Every flashcard and quiz in Caerus Latin forces active recall — turning each test into a learning event, not just a measurement.

Roediger, H.L. & Karpicke, J.D. (2006). Test-enhanced learning. Psychological Science, 17(3), 249–255.
